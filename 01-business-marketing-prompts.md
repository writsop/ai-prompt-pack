## Sales & Copywriting

### 1. Product Launch Email Sequence
```
You are a direct-response copywriter with 15 years of experience. Create a 5-email launch sequence for [PRODUCT]. 

Product: [describe product]
Target audience: [describe audience]
Price point: [price]
Key benefits: [list 3-5 benefits]
Launch date: [date]

For each email, provide:
- Subject line (with A/B variant)
- Preview text
- Full email body
- CTA button text
- Send timing relative to launch

Use the PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution) framework for emails 1-3, and urgency/scarcity for emails 4-5.
```

### 2. Landing Page Copy Generator
```
Write high-converting landing page copy for [PRODUCT/SERVICE].

Details:
- Product: [name and description]
- Target customer: [who they are]
- Main pain point solved: [problem]
- Unique selling proposition: [what makes it different]
- Price: [amount]
- Social proof available: [testimonials/stats]

Structure the page as:
1. Hero headline + subheadline (use the 4U formula: Urgent, Unique, Ultra-specific, Useful)
2. Problem section (3 pain points with emotional language)
3. Solution introduction
4. Feature-benefit pairs (6 features, each with a clear benefit)
5. Social proof section
6. Pricing with value stack
7. FAQ (5 objection-handling questions)
8. Final CTA with urgency element
```

### 3. Cold Email That Gets Replies
```
Write a cold outreach email to [PROSPECT TYPE] about [YOUR SERVICE].

Context:
- My company: [name and what you do]
- Target prospect: [their role, company type]
- Value I provide: [specific outcome]
- Proof point: [case study or metric]

Rules:
- Keep under 100 words
- No "I hope this email finds you well"
- Lead with their specific pain point
- Include one specific, relevant insight about their business
- End with a low-friction CTA (not "hop on a call")
- Write 3 variants with different angles
```

## Market Research

### 6. Competitor Analysis Framework
```
Act as a strategic market analyst. Create a comprehensive competitor analysis for [MY COMPANY] in the [INDUSTRY] space.

My company: [brief description]
Main competitors: [list 3-5 if known, or ask AI to identify them]
My target market: [description]

Analyze each competitor on:
1. Product/service offerings and pricing
2. Target audience and positioning
3. Unique value proposition
4. Marketing channels and messaging
5. Strengths and weaknesses
6. Customer sentiment (based on public reviews)
7. Market share estimate

Then provide:
- Gap analysis: What are competitors missing?
- Opportunity matrix: Where can I differentiate?
- Strategic recommendations: Top 3 actionable moves
```

### 8. Market Sizing Estimation

### 9. SWOT Analysis Generator
```
Conduct a thorough SWOT analysis for [BUSINESS/PROJECT].

Business: [description]
Industry: [sector]
Stage: [startup/growth/mature]
Recent developments: [any relevant context]

For each quadrant, provide at least 5 items with brief explanations:
- Strengths (internal positive)
- Weaknesses (internal negative)
- Opportunities (external positive)
- Threats (external negative)

Then create an action matrix:
- SO strategies (use strengths to capture opportunities)
- WO strategies (overcome weaknesses using opportunities)
- ST strategies (use strengths to mitigate threats)
- WT strategies (minimize weaknesses and avoid threats)

Prioritize the top 3 strategic actions.
```

### 31. Sales Call Script
```
Create a consultative sales call script for selling [PRODUCT/SERVICE].

Product: [description and price]
Target buyer: [role and company type]
Sales cycle: [length]
Common objections: [list if known]

Structure:
1. Opening (build rapport, set agenda - 2 min)
2. Discovery questions (understand their situation - 10 min)
   - Current state questions
   - Pain point questions
   - Impact questions
   - Future state questions
3. Presentation (connect solution to their needs - 10 min)
4. Social proof (relevant case study - 3 min)
5. Pricing discussion (value-first approach - 5 min)
6. Objection handling (top 5 objections with responses)
7. Close (clear next steps - 2 min)

Include transition phrases between each section.
```

### 32. Objection Handling Playbook
```
Create an objection handling playbook for [PRODUCT/SERVICE].

Product: [description]
Price: [amount]
Target buyer: [who]

For each of these common objections, provide 3 response strategies:

1. "It's too expensive"
2. "We need to think about it"
3. "We're already using [competitor]"
4. "We don't have budget right now"
5. "I need to talk to my [boss/partner/team]"
6. "Can you send me more information?"
7. "We tried something like this before and it didn't work"
8. "We're too busy to implement this right now"
9. "What makes you different from [competitor]?"
10. "We can build this in-house"

Response framework: Acknowledge → Ask → Answer → Advance
```
